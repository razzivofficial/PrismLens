Myopia (Nearsightedness)

Myopia is a common refractive error where distant objects appear blurry, but close objects can be seen clearly. It is often caused by a longer eyeball or a more curved cornea.

Causes, symptoms, and basic details about myopia can be explored in the following sections.

Hyperopia (Farsightedness)

Hyperopia is a refractive error where distant objects may be seen more clearly than close ones. It is caused by an eyeball that is too short or a cornea that has too little curvature.

Details about hyperopia can be explored in the following sections.

Color Blindness

Color blindness is a visual impairment that affects the perception of certain colors. The most common types are red-green and blue-yellow color blindness.
